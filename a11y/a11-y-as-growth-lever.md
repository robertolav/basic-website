Accessibility as a Growth Lever, Not a Compliance Burden
A Whitepaper for Leadership at Large Technology & Services Firms (e.g., Accenture)
Date: 21 November 2025

Executive Summary
Accessibility-first design is no longer a niche concern or a cost of compliance—it is a strategic growth lever that improves customer outcomes, unlocks new markets, reduces delivery risk, and strengthens brand equity. Evidence from UK Government Digital Service (GDS) monitoring shows accessibility interventions resolve thousands of issues and measurably improve public services’ usability; industry analyses link accessibility to material ROI through conversion gains, SEO uplift, and reduced support burden. Embedding accessibility across the delivery lifecycle can yield a ~$100 return per $1 invested while advancing inclusion for 16.1 million disabled people in the UK and many more experiencing situational or temporary impairments. The opportunity is to move from ad‑hoc compliance to an accessibility-first operating model: governance, KPIs, design system integration, and client co‑ownership.
Sources: GDS report & blog (Dec 2024) showing monitored scale, fixes and common issues; BOIA summarizing Forrester ROI; W3C WAI business case; Taylor Wessing on UK regulatory context. 1 [CodeInterpreter | PowerPoint], [duitdesign.com], [makeitfable.com], [accessibil…log.gov.uk]

1. The Strategic Business Case
1.1 Market Reach & Equity
The UK includes 16.1 million disabled people who depend on accessible services; accessibility also benefits older users, people in low bandwidth settings, and those with temporary constraints (e.g., injury). Inclusive design broadens reach and removes friction across segments, generating equitable access at scale.
Sources: GDS blog highlighting scale and mainstream benefits; W3C WAI articulating extended market reach and brand benefits. [CodeInterpreter | PowerPoint], [makeitfable.com]
1.2 Tangible ROI
Independent analyses estimate accessibility returns of ~$100 for every $1 invested via improved conversions, stronger SEO, and lower support overhead, with additional gains from reduced maintenance and faster innovation cycles when accessibility is embedded early in engineering.
Sources: BOIA summary of Forrester ROI; European Journal of CS & IT on improvements in satisfaction (+63%) and task completion (+37%). [duitdesign.com], [wppluginsify.com]
1.3 Legal & Reputational Risk Mitigation
Public sector bodies in the UK have a legal duty to meet accessibility standards under the Public Sector Bodies Accessibility Regulations (2018), and private sector responsibilities are shaped by the Equality Act 2010. Non‑compliance increases legal exposure, damages reputation, and erodes customer trust—risks that compound across large portfolios.
Sources: Taylor Wessing’s overview of UK regulatory framework; W3C WAI discussion of legal risk. [accessibil…log.gov.uk], [makeitfable.com]

2. Why Large Firms Struggle to Prioritize Accessibility-First
2.1 Framing Accessibility as Compliance, Not Strategy
Accessibility is frequently treated as a late‑stage checklist rather than a design and delivery principle, leading to rework and missed transformation benefits.
Sources: W3C WAI business case framing accessibility beyond compliance; industry commentary on ROI beyond legal duties. [makeitfable.com], [duitdesign.com]
2.2 Limited Visibility of ROI in Quarterly Metrics
Longer‑term gains (brand trust, reduced support, better UX) are diffuse and not consistently captured in performance dashboards, reinforcing short‑term trade‑offs.
Sources: BOIA/Forrester ROI discussion; W3C WAI on tangible and intangible benefits. [duitdesign.com], [makeitfable.com]
2.3 Delivery Pressures & Technical Debt
Compressed timelines, legacy platforms, and fear of regression make teams reluctant to retrofit accessibility, which is far costlier than building it in from the start.
Sources: GDS reports showing common pervasive issues (contrast, focus, keyboard, reflow); AbilityNet commentary on frequent pitfalls and remediation practices. 1 [gov.uk]
2.4 Skills & Ownership Gaps
Accessibility responsibilities are fragmented across UX, engineering, QA, and product without clear owners or skilled champions embedded in teams.
Sources: Fable on practical KPI frameworks and embedding progress; GDS insights on the importance of keeping statements up to date and integrating accessibility in lifecycle. [accessibil…arvard.edu], [CodeInterpreter | PowerPoint]
2.5 Procurement & Client Dynamics
If accessibility is not specified in RFPs or success criteria, suppliers deprioritize it to optimize cost/speed—an ecosystem challenge requiring client education and contractual alignment.
Sources: W3C WAI business case (procurement and risk); Taylor Wessing on legal environment shaping obligations. [makeitfable.com], [accessibil…log.gov.uk]

3. Evidence: What Works (UK Government & Industry)
3.1 UK GDS Monitoring Outcomes
Between Jan 2022–Sep 2024, GDS monitored 1,203 websites and 21 mobile apps, identified 29,787 issues, and 55% were resolved during monitoring; 68% of organisations fixed issues or had plans within 12 weeks—demonstrating that targeted audits and guided remediation drive rapid, measurable improvements.
Sources: GOV.UK corporate report; GDS blog summary. 1 [CodeInterpreter | PowerPoint]
3.2 Case Studies: Commercial Impact
Accessibility improvements have driven significant outcomes:
  • Tesco × RNIB: overhaul associated with +350% online grocery sales growth.
  • John Lewis & Partners: accessible checkout improved conversions and CX.
Sources: daily.dev case study compilation; Fable customer case studies. [umavi.com], [daily.dev]

4. Operating Model for Accessibility‑First
4.1 Governance & Accountability
  • Executive Mandate: Adopt a board‑level policy: Accessibility‑First by Design.
  • Clear Ownership: Assign product owners responsible for accessibility outcomes; embed Accessibility Champions in each squad.
  • Independent Assurance: Annual third‑party audits; quarterly KPI reporting into risk/governance forums.
Sources: W3C WAI guidance on integrating accessibility; Fable’s KPI framework for ongoing measurement. [makeitfable.com], [accessibil…arvard.edu]
4.2 KPIs That Leadership Can Track
  • Coverage: % of critical journeys/components passing WCAG 2.1 AA (automated + manual).
  • Outcome: Task success, time‑on‑task, error rates for assistive tech users.
  • Quality & Cost: Defect density, rework hours avoided due to early reviews; support ticket volume related to accessibility.
  • Capability: % squads with trained champions; cadence of audits; accessibility statement currency.
Sources: Fable KPI framework; GDS monitoring emphasis on statements and common issues. [accessibil…arvard.edu], [CodeInterpreter | PowerPoint]
4.3 Delivery Lifecycle Integration
  • Design System: Bake accessibility into components (semantic structure, visible focus, keyboard operability, contrast).
  • Definition of Done: Accessibility criteria at story level; automated checks (e.g., axe) in CI; targeted manual testing with assistive tech users.
  • User Research: Recruit disabled users and assistive tech users for continuous testing to validate real‑world usability.
Sources: GOV.UK monitoring & common issues; AbilityNet guidance; Fable case practice of testing with assistive tech users. 1 [gov.uk], [daily.dev]

5. Implementation Roadmap (180 Days)
0–30 Days
  • Executive endorsement and policy issuance; appoint Accessibility Champions; baseline accessibility statements across products.
  • Select pilot services and set KPIs; integrate automated checks in CI.
Sources: GDS report emphasizing statements and systematic monitoring; Fable KPI framework. 1 [accessibil…arvard.edu]
30–90 Days
  • Design system updates: contrast, focus states, keyboard operability, scalable typography; caption/transcripts for video.
  • Begin assisted‑tech user testing cycles; publish remediation plans and progress.
Sources: AbilityNet common issues & fixes; W3C WAI business case for practical benefits. [gov.uk], [makeitfable.com]
90–180 Days
  • Extend to top 10 user journeys per portfolio; embed Definition of Done; quarterly KPI reporting to leadership.
  • Establish annual independent audit schedule; include accessibility requirements in procurement templates and RFPs.
Sources: W3C WAI on procurement and governance; Taylor Wessing on regulatory considerations. [makeitfable.com], [accessibil…log.gov.uk]

  • Lost Conversions & Support Overhead: Barriers drive abandonment and inflate support tickets.
Sources: W3C WAI risk framing; industry evidence linking accessibility to improved performance and reduced costs. [makeitfable.com], [duitdesign.com]

7. Appendices
A. UK Landscape & Digital Inclusion
Digital exclusion still impacts millions in the UK—16% of adults lack foundation-level digital skills and 4% are offline—making accessible, user‑centred services critical.

B. Common Technical Barriers & Quick Fixes
  • Contrast & Focus: Ensure 4.5:1 contrast, visible focus states.
  • Keyboard Operability: All interactive elements accessible by keyboard alone.
  • Reflow & Responsiveness: Content adjusts without loss.
Sources: GDS monitoring common issues; AbilityNet testing insights. 1 [gov.uk]
