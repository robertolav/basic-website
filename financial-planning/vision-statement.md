Vision → Goals → Strategy → Expected Result & Benefits

Vision
To build a shared financial strategy that aligns with our family values, ensures security, and supports long-term aspirations—moving from separate, reactive money management to joint, intentional planning.

Goals
Individual (Advisor Meeting):
  • Understand current financial position (income, expenses, savings, pensions).
  • Define personal priorities: retirement age, emergency fund, debt-free living, family experiences.
  • Translate US concepts from Ramit Sethi’s system into UK equivalents (ISA, pensions, credit rating).
Joint (With Mary):
  • Agree on a shared vision for money: security, freedom, experiences.
  • Create a combined monthly budget with flexibility for personal spending.
  • Decide on joint savings/investment accounts for shared goals.
  • Align on risk tolerance and investment approach.

Strategy
  1. Clarify Numbers: Income, expenses, net worth, pensions.
  2. Set Up Core Accounts:
    ○ Current account for bills/spending.
    ○ Emergency fund (3–6 months expenses).
    ○ Stocks & Shares ISA for tax-free investing.
    ○ Maximize pension contributions.
  3. Automate: Transfers to savings/investments post-payday.
  4. Joint Planning:
    ○ Monthly “Money Date” to review goals and progress.
    ○ Start with values, then move to numbers.
  5. Advisor Role:
    ○ Optimize pensions, ISAs, and tax efficiency.
    ○ Create roadmap for retirement and big goals.

Expected Results & Benefits
  • Shared understanding and alignment on money.
  • Automated, stress-free system for saving, investing, and spending.
  • Clear roadmap for retirement, family experiences, and financial freedom.
  • Stronger partnership and reduced financial stress.
